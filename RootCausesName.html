Root cause #1: Financial literacy

Evidence

Twenty-three states require financial literacy classes, and twenty-five states require economics classes.

Explanation

Almost half of Americans do not receive financial literacy education. Some places that do have financial literacy classes do not teach effectively for the students, so states decide not to implement them.

Evidence

People make avoidable financial mistakes.

Explanation

People do not have the resources or knowledge that they need to avoid these mistakes.

Implications

More states can require financial literacy classes. However, they can show limited benefits, so classes should be more interactive, like having stock market simulations.
